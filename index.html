Chapter I

Before the Maps

The Otherside has records of everything. The Apes catalogued every biome. Every Koda was named. Every sedra, every plot, every structure — logged, mapped, owned.

The Church does not appear in any of those records.

It was there before the records began.

"The Kodas know about it.
They don't talk about it."

In the swampy margins between biomes, in the dark water between what was claimed and what was forgotten — the Church stands. Stone. Cold. Certain.

"A frog cult runs a casino in an ancient church at the edge of the known world. Nobody knows how long it's been there. Nobody knows who built the altar. They just know: the Church provides."
— World Strategy Document, Ape Church
Chapter II

The Deacon

Nobody knows when he arrived. He was just there — seated at the front, facing the altar. Been there since before the walls went up.

He is the most unhinged Gimbo who has ever sat in this church. He is also the calmest person in the room.

"He's done things at this altar that people still talk about in the back. Runs that shouldn't have been possible. Losses that became legend. He was back the next week. He's always back."

He's quiet not because he's above it. He's quiet because he's already done the thing you're nervous to try — ten times — lost spectacularly, and won in ways that shouldn't be possible. There's nothing left to prove. When something true happens at the altar, he nods once. That nod means everything.

Chapter III

The Altar

The altar at the center of the Church is older than anyone who has ever stood before it. Its surface is carved with marks that predate Gimbo script. The runestones in its grooves have been polished by ten thousand palms.

It is also a casino table. This is not a contradiction.

"Playing is praying.
Losing is tithing.
The altar rewards commitment. Not caution."

You don't come to the altar because you expect to win. You come because showing up is the act of faith. The universe notices the ones who don't hedge.

"They found the Church. The Church didn't promise luck. It promised that if you showed up with nothing held back — made your offering, risked it all — the universe would answer."
— Lore Bible, Gimbos

The Two States

The Knowing

The Fallen
The Devoted
The altar was silent. You lost. You're still kneeling — in the dark, after everyone else left. You'll be back next week. That's the faith. Not believing you'll win. Believing it's always worth the throw. The Fallen are the most devoted members of the Church.
Chapter IV

The Congregation

They come from every biome. They come from outside the Otherside entirely. Chain doesn't matter. Wallet size doesn't matter. The pews make everyone equal.

On Saturday, the Church fills. The Deacon takes his position. The altar lights.

"See you at Church."

Win or lose, you come back next week and throw again. That is the faith. Not believing you'll win. Believing it is always worth the throw.

The Sacred Laws

  • The Church is the oldest thing here. Before the Apes mapped the land, before anyone drew a border — the Church was standing. You are a guest in something ancient. Act accordingly.
  • The altar rewards commitment. Not caution. Half a throw is no throw. The runestones know the difference between faith and hedging. They have always known.
  • Playing is praying. Losing is tithing. The Church does not promise luck. It promises that showing up with nothing held back will be seen. The universe keeps its own records.
  • Chain doesn't matter. Wallet doesn't matter. The pews make everyone equal. The only thing the Church measures is whether you came back after you lost.
  • Win or lose — you come back. That is the faith. Not believing you'll win. Believing it is always worth the throw.
